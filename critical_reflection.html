Critical Reflection/Analysis

The DIEP framework was used to structure this reflection!


Describe

My work placement took place in Securestack company which is one of the top 20 cybersecurity startups to watch in 2021. The main purpose of this placement is to build a lightweight penetration testing web application that allows users to successfully scan a website and get its network security test results which include both the vulnerabilities and strengths of the website. The whole placement had to be completed by week 12 of this trimester. To complete this medium size project in a short period of given time, 4 students were engaged in this project from the front-end to the back-end of web design. My role was to build penetration testing logics and tools for the back-end design using Python programming language. On my first meeting with Securestack company, the senior developer had clearly explained all the processes and objectives of this placement; tools and required software were installed on the same day. During the placement, we normally had a morning meeting to track and follow up with the project's progress. All team members were working hard and built good communication; thus, everything was completed as planned, and we were able to hand in our project on time.


Interpret

Personally, I feel that the whole procedures of this placement were brilliantly organised by Securestack company to help the intern students to break the gap between the theoretical knowledge that were taught in class and the real-life Software Development Life Cycle. Before this placement, I had limited knowledge in Python programming language and generic skills, such as Version Control and Docker Engine. However, with the support and mentor from Securestack's senior developer, I understood what an IT professional should really do at each phase of the project life cycle, and I also noticed my skill sets were improved quickly overtime. In addition, Securestack offered some tutorials about new technologies at each milestone of the project. The company also brought the team members together and encouraged us to share ideas and speak openly to figure out how we could develop solutions to problems. As an IT practitioner, I can tell that this is a priceless experience for me to gain in-depth knowledge in the computer science industry; and it will be very beneficial to my future career.


Evaluate

One of the most personally rewarding aspects of my placement experience was the opportunity to work as a team in a real company. Before, I used to think that teamwork is not an effective way to complete projects. It was not something that I had previously liked to practise in class because I had more control over my projects if I did them alone. However, this placement experience has changed my perception of teamwork and helped me to understand why it is so important to work as a team to achieve the goal. From working within a team, I think that good communication in this industry underpins almost everything that an IT professional does, and it can determine the quality of products that customers will receive. On top of that, I have improved my transferable skills, such as critical thinking, decision making, and problem-solving, through team discussions. This made me become passionate to work with my team members so that we could compensate for each other weaknesses and worked toward success.


Plan

Reflecting on my 12 weeks of experience with Securestack company, I have gained an immense amount of knowledge in both technical and transferable skills. It has opened a path and guided my way to become an IT professional soon. To improve my knowledge in both areas, I usually spend one hour every morning (because human brains are fresh at that time) to study new generic skills or watch TED Talks to motivate my career aspirations. In addition, I plan on joining ACS Australia where I can continue to develop my skills and build networks with other senior developers which is very beneficial to my future professional life.
